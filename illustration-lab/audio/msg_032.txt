Here's my best idea: one "review boards" surface, fed by all your chats.

The shared store already exists — it's the Firebase project that both ImageForge and the journal app already point at. Each chat writes a "board": its images or video go to storage, plus a small manifest to the database — a boards entry per chat, with its items and a tiny display config.

Then: one viewer, with a tab per chat. The key trick is that each board carries its own config — which labels to show, grid versus swipe, image versus video. So the movies chat declares video and its own columns, this chat declares grids and model and cost — same app, different requirements handled by data, not by new code. That's what keeps "one tab per chat" clean instead of a maintenance mess.

It lives in the iOS app — a new "Boards" tab in the JournalReader app you already have on TestFlight — with a web mirror for desktop. And your Tinder swipe-to-curate idea from way back just becomes one of the board modes.

I'd build it incrementally so it's not one giant bang. One: nail the manifest schema. Two: a tiny "post to board" helper any chat can drop in.
