Sure. When I say formatting, I mean only Notion's own markup, never your actual writing.

So: the little stars Notion uses for bold and italic, the hashtags in front of a heading, the layout tags for columns and synced blocks, the long image links, which I turned into a simple "pic" marker so you know a drawing was there, the color tags that tint text, the gray divider lines, and the three dashes you used as page breaks. I used those dashes to decide where one band ends and the next begins, and then removed the dashes themselves. I also cleaned up backslashes that Notion adds in front of brackets and other punctuation.

What I kept is everything you actually wrote. Every word, your punctuation, your spelling, your typos, your own pic notes, your arrows, the missing markers, all of it, exactly as is.

The one honest tradeoff: flattening the spacing also flattens your layouts. So when you'd arranged a note like a little diagram, with things in columns and arrows between them, it becomes a straight run of lines instead. All the words are still there, but the way they were arranged on the page is not. And a drawing shows up as the word pic in parentheses, not the actual picture.
